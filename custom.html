Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our custom jewelry process

How long does the custom jewelry process take?

The timeline for custom jewelry varies depending on complexity, but typically takes 4-8 weeks from initial consultation to final delivery. Simple designs may be completed in 2-3 weeks, while intricate pieces with multiple gemstones can take 8-12 weeks.

We provide a detailed timeline during your consultation and keep you updated throughout the process with progress photos and regular communication.

What is your design consultation fee?

We charge a KES 5,000 design consultation fee which is fully credited toward your final purchase. This fee covers the time our master designers spend creating detailed sketches, 3D renderings, and sourcing appropriate materials for your project.

The consultation fee ensures we can dedicate the necessary time and resources to bring your vision to life with the attention to detail it deserves.

Can I use my own gemstones or heirlooms?

Yes, we frequently work with client-provided gemstones and heirloom pieces. We begin with a thorough assessment of your materials to ensure they're suitable for the intended design.

Please note that we cannot be responsible for pre-existing damage to client-provided stones, and we recommend insurance for valuable heirlooms during the crafting process.

Do you provide certificates for gemstones?

All our diamonds over 0.5 carats and colored gemstones over 1 carat come with certification from recognized gemological laboratories. We provide full documentation for all precious materials used in your custom piece.

For smaller stones, we provide detailed specifications but may not include formal certification unless specifically requested (additional fees may apply).

What is your revision policy during the design phase?

We include up to three rounds of revisions in our design process at no additional cost. This allows you to refine the details until you're completely satisfied with the design.

Major conceptual changes after the third revision may incur additional design fees, which we'll discuss transparently before proceeding.
